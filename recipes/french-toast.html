French toast

Description

French toast is traditionally made with day-old slices because they absorb the eggy mixture better than fresh ones (and this method prevents waste â€“ it's a win-win). Of course, a slightly stale loaf is certainly not a requirement. If you only have fresh bread on hand, that'll work just fine.

  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Vanilla and cinnamon
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • white Bread

How to make French toast

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  3. Lighly butter a griddle or skillet and heat over medium-high heat.
  4. Dunk bread in teh egg mixture, soaking both sides.
  5. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  6. Serve hot.
